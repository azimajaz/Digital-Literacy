# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

---

## ✅ General Online Safety

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. Use a password manager (like Bitwarden or Google Password Manager) instead of reusing the same password across sites.

- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, and banking apps. Prefer an authenticator app over SMS-based OTP when possible.

- [ ] **Never click suspicious links** in SMS, WhatsApp, or email — even if the sender appears to be your bank, college, or a delivery service. Go directly to the official website instead.

- [ ] **Keep your devices and apps updated.** Security patches in updates protect you from known vulnerabilities that attackers actively exploit.

- [ ] **Avoid using public/free Wi-Fi** for sensitive activities like banking, logging into college portals, or sharing personal information. Use mobile data or a VPN instead.

- [ ] **Check app permissions regularly.** Revoke access for any app that requests unnecessary permissions (e.g., a flashlight app asking for contacts or microphone access).

---

## 💳 Financial & UPI Safety (Specific Tips)

- [ ] **Remember: Receiving money NEVER requires your UPI PIN.** You only enter your PIN to *send* money. If anyone asks you to enter your PIN to receive a payment, it is a scam — hang up immediately.

- [ ] **Never scan a QR code sent by a buyer/stranger.** On platforms like OLX or Facebook Marketplace, scammers send QR codes that *debit* your account. The sender of money should never need to scan a code.

- [ ] **Do not install remote-access apps** (AnyDesk, TeamViewer, AnyConnect) at the request of anyone claiming to be from your bank or a customer support team. Legitimate banks never ask for this.

- [ ] **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your banking/UPI app settings to minimize potential losses in case of fraud.

---

## 🔒 Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] **Do not share personal details publicly** — Aadhaar number, PAN, date of birth, home address, or phone number — on social media profiles or public forums.

- [ ] **Be cautious of urgent requests** from friends on social media asking for money. Accounts are frequently hacked; always verify via a phone call before transferring any amount.

---

## 🚨 Reporting Cybercrime

If you or someone you know has been a victim of cybercrime:

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Helpline** | Call **1930** (available 24×7) |
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | File a complaint at [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) |
| **RBI Ombudsman** | For banking/UPI fraud: [cms.rbi.org.in](https://cms.rbi.org.in) |
| **Local Police** | File an FIR at your nearest cybercrime police station |

> **Tip:** Report as quickly as possible. The sooner you report UPI fraud, the higher the chance of freezing the transaction.
