# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud
**Cybercrime Awareness**

---

## The Crime

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India. Attackers exploit the trust and convenience of platforms like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm to deceive victims into authorizing fraudulent transactions.

---

## How It Typically Happens

1. **Contact:** The fraudster contacts the victim via phone call, SMS, or WhatsApp, posing as a bank official, e-commerce customer support agent, or a buyer on OLX/Quikr.
2. **Hook:** They claim the victim has a pending refund, KYC update, or payment issue that must be resolved immediately.
3. **Deception:** The scammer sends a *collect request* (a debit request) on the victim's UPI app, but verbally describes it as a "payment you'll receive."
4. **Authorization:** The victim, believing they are receiving money, enters their UPI PIN — which actually *sends* money to the attacker.
5. **Escalation:** In some cases, the fraudster installs a remote-access app (like AnyDesk) to take control of the device and drain multiple accounts.

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted

- First-time UPI users and senior citizens unfamiliar with digital payments
- College students selling items on second-hand marketplaces
- Small business owners expecting payments from customers
- People who recently filed complaints with banks or e-commerce platforms

---

## Consequences

- **Financial:** Victims lose anywhere from ₹500 to several lakhs; recovery is rarely guaranteed.
- **Emotional:** Significant stress, anxiety, and loss of trust in digital financial systems.
- **Legal complexity:** Reversing UPI transactions is difficult; disputes can take months.
- **Data risk:** If remote-access tools were installed, additional personal and banking data may be compromised.
